Pickleball’s Popularity Peaks in Predominantly White States

Data reveals the sport thrives in states where white populations make up at least 50%

By Erin Maher

December 22, 2024

Pickleball, the fast-paced paddle sport taking the nation by storm, has cemented its place as America’s fastest-growing sport, according to the 2024 Sports & Fitness Industry Association (SFIA) Topline Participation Report.

With nearly 41,000 courts across the U.S., as reported by USA Pickleball, the sport’s national governing body, pickleball is a phenomenon of unprecedented growth. While states like California, Florida, and Texas boast the highest total number of courts—each exceeding 2,000—they fall behind when it comes to courts per capita.

Surprisingly, pickleball's popularity shines brightest in states with predominantly white populations. Data from USA Pickleball, combined with U.S. Census Bureau statistics, reveals that states like Maine, Utah, Vermont, and Wyoming, where white populations exceed 75%, lead the nation in courts per capita.
